Marina N. Sharifi

I am currently completing a fellowship in Medical Oncology at the University of Wisconsin, Madison. I'm in the ABIM research pathway which gives me the opportunity for additional dedicated research time during my fellowship.

I completed my MD/PhD in June 2016 at the University of Chicago MSTP program. I did my graduate work in the Macleod Lab in the Committee on Cancer Biology and Ben May Department of Cancer Research, studying the role of autophagy in metastasis.

I graduated from the University of California, Berkeley in 2006 with BA degrees in Molecular and Cell Biology and German Literature. From 2006 to 2008 I worked as a technician and lab manager in the Niyogi Lab in the Department of Plant and Microbial Biology at UC Berkeley.

PhD Research

Autophagy and tumor cell metastasis

Macro-autophagy is a catabolic process important for degradation of damaged organelles, protein aggregates and intracellular recycling of proteins and lipids to help keep cells healthy. It is co-opted by tumor cells to promote survival during nutrient stress, hypoxia, and exposure to cytotoxic therapy within the primary tumor, but whether autophagy impacts the metastatic process is less well understood. My research focused on investigating whether inhibition of autophagy affected the survival and metastatic potential of metastatic tumor cells, primarily in a mouse model of aggressive breast cancer. I found that not only does inhibition of autophagy inhibit metastatic behavior in vitro and metastasis in vivo, it does so through a novel direct function in tumor cell migration. Read more here.
